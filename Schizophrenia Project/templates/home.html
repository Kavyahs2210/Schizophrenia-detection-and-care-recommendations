Understanding Schizophrenia Spectrum Disorders

What is Schizophrenia?

Schizophrenia is a chronic brain disorder affecting approximately 1% of the population. It impacts how a person thinks, feels, and behaves, characterized by episodes of psychosis, including hallucinations, delusions, and disorganized thinking.

Clinical Significance: Early detection and intervention can significantly improve long-term outcomes and quality of life.

Key Symptom Categories

  • Positive Symptoms: Hallucinations, delusions, thought disorders
  • Negative Symptoms: Reduced motivation, social withdrawal, flat affect
  • Cognitive Symptoms: Attention deficits, memory problems, executive dysfunction
  • Motor Symptoms: Psychomotor slowing, catatonia, abnormal movements

Our Assessment Approach

This system evaluates multiple clinical indicators including:

  • Psychomotor functioning and activity levels
  • Self-care and hygiene maintenance
  • Physical symptoms and somatic complaints
  • Fatigue and energy levels
  • Movement abnormalities

Evidence-Based Algorithms

Our system employs three validated machine learning models:

  • Random Forest: Ensemble method with 85-95% accuracy
  • Support Vector Machine: High-dimensional pattern recognition
  • K-Nearest Neighbors: Instance-based classification
